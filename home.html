THE BEST TIME PERIODS FOR TURTLE WATCHING

In Sri Lanka, different species of turtles nest and hatch at different times throughout the year. The best time periods to watch turtles in Sri Lanka depend on the specific turtle species you're interested in observing.

  1. Leatherback Sea Turtles:

    Leatherback turtles typically nest from November to March along the southern and eastern coasts of Sri Lanka. The peak nesting season is usually from December to January.

  2. Green Sea Turtles:

    Green turtles can be observed nesting in Sri Lanka from January to July, with the peak nesting season occurring from March to June. The beaches of Rekawa and Kosgoda are known for green turtle nesting activity.

  3. Hawksbill Sea Turtles:

    Hawksbill turtles nest in Sri Lanka from October to April, with the peak nesting season falling between November and January. The beaches of Pigeon Island, Kosgoda, and Rekawa are important nesting sites for hawksbill turtles.

  4. Loggerhead Sea Turtles:

    Loggerhead turtles are rare visitors to Sri Lanka's shores. Their nesting period is less defined, but sightings have been reported from time to time.

  5. Olive Ridley Sea Turtles:

    Olive Ridley turtles nest in Sri Lanka, but their nesting patterns are less predictable compared to other species. Nesting events can occur throughout the year, with higher activity reported from February to July.

HIKKADUWA TURTLE BEACH

Hikkaduwa Beach, located on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, is a popular tourist attraction known for its pristine sandy shores and turquoise waters. It offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, vibrant marine life, and a laid-back atmosphere that attracts visitors from around the world.

The beach is renowned for its fantastic snorkeling and diving opportunities, allowing visitors to explore the colorful coral reefs and encounter an array of tropical fish and other marine species. With its clear waters and abundant underwater life, Hikkaduwa Beach is a paradise for nature enthusiasts and underwater adventurers.

THINGS YOU SHOULD AVOID DOING AT A TURTLE HATCHERY

When turtle watching, it's important to respect and protect these creatures and their natural environment.

  • Disturbing nesting sites.
  • Touching or handling turtles.
  • Flash photography.
  • Harassing turtles.
  • Removing or disturbing turtle eggs.
  • Feeding turtles human food.
