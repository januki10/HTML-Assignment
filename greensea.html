Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas)

The Green Sea Turtle is the most commonly found turtle category in Sri Lankan waters. Growing to a maximum length of 1m and weighing about 250kg, an adult female can lay between 120 to 40 eggs at a time.Green sea Turtle can be recognized from its flattened body covered by a tear shaped shell that is blackish grey in colour, a smaller head than the loggerhead turtle and a pair of large paddle like flippers.They are found mainly thriving in the tropical waters of the ocean, feeding only on marine vegetation such as algae and sea grass, returning to the shore to lay eggs.
