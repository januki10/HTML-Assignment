Sri Lanka is a heaven for sea turtles, with several beaches serving as important nesting sites for these incredible creatures. One such beach is the world-famous Rekawa Beach, located on the southern coast of the island. Rekawa Beach is renowned for its annual turtle nesting season, attracting visitors and conservationists alike. Five species of sea turtles, including the Green Sea Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle, can be found nesting on this beach. Witnessing the mesmerizing sight of baby turtles hatching and making their way to the ocean is an unforgettable experience.

In addition to Rekawa Beach, other beaches in Sri Lanka, such as Kosgoda, Induruwa, and Pigeon Island, also serve as vital turtle nesting sites. These beaches provide ideal conditions for sea turtles to lay their eggs, with warm sand and ample space for nesting. Strict conservation efforts have been implemented to protect these nesting grounds, ensuring the safety of the eggs and hatchlings. Local conservation projects and hatcheries play a crucial role in monitoring and safeguarding the nests, as well as raising awareness about the importance of turtle conservation.

The hatching process of sea turtles in Sri Lanka is a delicate and fascinating journey. After an incubation period of around 45 to 70 days, the baby turtles start to emerge from their nests. With an instinctive drive, they make their way to the ocean, guided by the natural light and sounds of the waves. This critical journey is perilous for the hatchlings, as they face numerous predators and obstacles along the sandy shores. However, with the dedicated efforts of conservationists and volunteers, many hatchlings are carefully guided and protected, increasing their chances of survival and contributing to the preservation of these remarkable creatures in Sri Lanka's coastal ecosystems.
